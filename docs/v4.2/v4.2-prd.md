# Emoji Life v4.2 PRD — Gene Balancing & Visual System

**Status:** Draft
**Version:** 4.2.0
**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Breaking:** No — backwards compatible with v4.x saves

---

## 1. Overview

v4.2 is a focused update that brings gene balancing improvements and visual system enhancements to Emoji Life. The unified organism model, phenotype classification, and other features originally scoped for v5 are deferred indefinitely.

### Goals

- Self-balancing genetics through trade-offs, not hard limits
- Continuous-only trait expression (remove hard ceilings)
- Universal animation system for action indication
- Configurable indicator slots for per-agent visual information
- Generalized directional tool rendering
- Geographic isolation through terrain for population divergence
- Improved pregnancy mechanic with gradual need transfer
- Leveling energy cost scaling for natural power balance
- Backward compatibility with v4.x save files

### Non-Goals

- Unified organism model (trees remain grid objects)
- Phenotype classification system
- New entity types (plants-as-agents, fungi, sessile predators, etc.)
- Meat/plant food type split
- Corpse/kill-before-harvest mechanic
- New traits for Body & Physiology (Size, Regeneration)
- New traits for Mobility, Senses & Diet (Aquatic, Saltwater Tolerance, Photosynthesis, Carnivory)
- New traits for Mind & Behavior (Emotion as a gene, Recall as AN)
- Functional minimum thresholds system
- Breaking changes to save format

## 2. Core Design Principles

### 2.1 Trade-off-Driven Genetics

Every trait has paired costs. Higher Strength → higher attack energy cost. Higher Agility → higher movement energy cost. Higher Fertility → more demanding reproduction. The cumulative cost of many high traits creates an **emergent energy budget**: an agent with everything maxed would starve. Natural selection emerges from the economy, not arbitrary caps.

**v4 current:** `value = clamp(default + (direction × raw / scale), min, max)` — hard min/max bounds.

**v4.2:** No hard ceilings. Traits scale freely from a soft floor of zero (or other functional minimum). Cost functions scale with trait values, making extreme values self-limiting.

### 2.2 All Traits Are Continuous

There is **no distinction between "capability" and "continuous" traits**. Every trait is a continuous value. What v4 modeled as binary capabilities (can attack, can move, can socialize) become continuous traits with a **functional minimum** — a value at or below which the capability is effectively unexpressed.

**Example:** An agent with Strength at 0 cannot effectively attack. At low values, attacks deal minimal damage but cost energy. At high values, attacks are devastating but expensive. The capability exists on a continuum, not as a switch.

This pattern applies to combat, mobility, leveling, sociality, etc. **Exception:** Parthenogenesis (TT) remains boolean in v4.2 — any positive gene expression enables asexual reproduction. Converting it to a continuous trait is deferred.

## 3. Genetics System

### 3.1 Trade-off Philosophy

**Primary mechanism: Paired costs.** Every trait has an explicit cost dimension — typically increased energy cost for the actions or subsystems enabled by that trait. A strong agent pays more energy per attack. A fast agent pays more energy per move. An agent with keen recall pays passive energy every tick.

**Secondary mechanism: Pleiotropy.** Some traits affect multiple stats simultaneously, with mixed positive/negative effects. Future traits (e.g., Size) may increase attack power AND max HP AND inventory capacity, but also increase food needs AND decrease movement speed.

**Emergent mechanism: Energy budget.** The cumulative effect of paired costs means an agent with many high traits has proportionally high total energy costs. An agent with maxed Strength, Agility, Recall, and Resilience would starve unless it also evolved exceptional foraging ability — which itself has costs. This self-balancing loop replaces v4's hard min/max caps. No artificial point budget is needed; the energy economy IS the budget.

### 3.2 Expression Changes

**v4 current:** `value = clamp(default + (direction × raw / scale), min, max)`

**v4.2:**
- **Soft floor only:** Traits cannot go below their functional minimum (often 0)
- **No hard ceilings:** Traits scale freely with accumulated gene expression
- **Cost scaling:** Each trait's paired cost scales with its expressed value (linear, quadratic, or other curves — TRD decides per-trait)

### 3.3 Trait Naming and Disambiguation

To prevent confusion between related traits, here is the canonical glossary. Every trait belongs to exactly one concept. Traits marked **(new)** are introduced in v4.2; all others carry over from v4.

| Trait | Concept | NOT to be confused with |
|-------|---------|-------------------------|
| **Strength** | Physical combat power and attack damage | Resilience (HP) |
| **Vigor** | Energy pool size | Endurance (carrying capacity), Metabolism (consumption rate) |

### 3.4 Trait Catalog

#### 3.4.1 Essential Traits

Required for viability. An agent must have at least one coding gene for each essential trait or it is stillborn at birth. In v4, there are **five** essential traits:

| Code | Trait | What It Does | Trade-off |
|------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| AA | **Strength** | Base attack damage and per-level scaling | Higher attack energy cost per strike (v4.2 addition) |
| BB | **Longevity** | Maximum lifespan in milliseconds | Higher lifespan → higher base fullness decay (maintaining a long-lived body costs more) |
| CC | **Vigor** | Maximum energy pool and per-level scaling | Higher passive energy drain to maintain reserves (v4.2 addition) |
| DD | **Metabolism** | Fullness decay rate AND action speed multiplier | Faster actions and more energetic existence → proportionally higher fullness decay |
| EE | **Resilience** | Maximum HP and per-level scaling | Higher fullness cost to maintain a durable body (v4.2 addition) |

In addition, every viable agent must have a functional energy source (eating food, which is enabled by default). Viability also requires that no essential trait is at its absolute minimum — see Section 3.5.

#### 3.4.2 Social

Continuous traits affecting interaction with other agents. Note: Sociality is the master "social capability" trait — at zero, the agent cannot form factions, has no social need, and never selects social actions.

| Code | Trait | Benefit | Trade-off |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| AD | **Sociality** | Controls social need decay rate (higher = faster decay = stronger drive to interact). Enables faction formation and social actions. At zero, agent is fully solitary — no factions, no social actions, no loneliness. **Replaces Gregariousness (OO); same component (`socialDecay`) with a new gene code.** | Higher social need decay means the agent must socialize more frequently or suffer need penalties. Time spent socializing is time not spent on survival or reproduction. |
| NN | **Charisma** | More relationship slots | Higher social need decay (maintaining many relationships is draining) |
| II | **Cooperation** | Higher probability of positive social outcomes (sharing, helping, alliance formation) | Reduced combat effectiveness (non-confrontational instincts) |
| SS | **Fidelity** | Less likely to leave faction, loyal to allies | Cannot easily abandon failing or disadvantaged factions, even when leaving would be beneficial |

#### 3.4.3 Reproduction & Lifecycle

Continuous traits affecting how the agent produces and cares for offspring.

| Code | Trait | Benefit | Trade-off |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| LL | **Fertility** | Lower energy threshold to reproduce, stronger reproductive urge | Higher energy cost per reproduction event |
| AG | **Pregnancy** | Length of gestation period in milliseconds. At zero, birth is instant after conception. Higher values trade time for offspring quality — see notes below. **(New gene)** | During pregnancy, the parent's needs decay faster because the gestating offspring's needs are being filled by drawing from the parent (see Section 5.2 for full mechanic). The parent is also vulnerable: reduced speed, reduced combat ability. |
| TT | **Parthenogenesis** | Enables asexual reproduction (no mate required). **Boolean trait:** any positive gene expression enables asexual reproduction; this is not a continuous value. | Offspring receive no crossover (only mutation), reducing genetic diversity. Lineages converge faster, becoming vulnerable to environmental shifts. |
| VV | **Maternity** | Higher probability of feeding and caring for own offspring (the parent shares food with the child after birth) | Energy and resources spent on offspring instead of self |
| QQ | **Maturity** | Shorter juvenile phase (faster transition to adult) | Reduced initial adult stats — an agent that grew up too fast starts adulthood smaller and weaker, taking longer to reach peak via XP |
| AP | **Volatility** | Higher mutation rate applied to offspring DNA, accelerating evolutionary change. **(New gene)** | Higher rate of stillborn offspring (mutations frequently break essential genes). High-volatility lineages evolve fast but lose more children. |

#### 3.4.4 Resource Behavior

| Code | Trait | Benefit | Trade-off |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| PP | **Appetite** | Seeks food at higher fullness (proactive foraging — never gets caught hungry) | Consumes more food per meal (bigger appetite uses more resources per eating action) |
| UU | **Greed** | Stronger hoarding drive — accumulates more inventory before stopping | Less likely to share with others; weakens alliance formation |

### 3.5 Viability Rules

An agent is **viable** (not stillborn) if all of the following are true:

1. Has at least one coding gene for **each** essential trait: AA (Strength), BB (Longevity), CC (Vigor), DD (Metabolism), EE (Resilience)
2. No essential trait is at its absolute minimum value:
   - Strength `baseAttack > 3` (min)
   - Longevity `maxAgeMs > 120000` (min)
   - Vigor `baseMaxEnergy > 100` (min)
   - Metabolism `fullnessDecay < 0.06` (max — inverted trait, so "worst case" is max)
   - Resilience `baseMaxHp > 60` (min)
3. DNA length is within valid bounds (v4 uses [100, 250] characters)

Stillborn agents are not added to the world. Their parent loses the energy spent on reproduction but no child entity exists.

**Stillbirth is purely viability-driven.** There is no separate stillbirth probability roll based on Volatility or any other trait. High Volatility produces more stillbirths only because more mutations break essential genes more often, causing the viability check above to fail. This means lineages that build genomic robustness through gene duplication can absorb more mutations without losing offspring — a natural and emergent reward for redundancy.

### 3.6 Crossover & Mutation

Inherited from v4 unchanged in mechanism, with one v4.2 enhancement:

- **Crossover** (`crossover.ts`): positional gene swap between two parent DNA strings, 50% chance per gene position. Used for sexual reproduction.
- **Mutation** (`mutation.ts`): per-character substitution, gene duplication, gene deletion. Length clamped to valid bounds.
- **v4.2 enhancement:** The mutation rate applied to offspring is now derived from the parents' **Volatility** trait, not a global constant. For sexual reproduction, child mutation rate = average of both parents' Volatility expressions. For asexual, it equals the single parent's Volatility. The exact mapping (Volatility value → mutation probability) is a TRD concern.

The base global mutation rate from v4 (0.5% per character) becomes the rate at default Volatility expression. High-Volatility lineages mutate faster; low-Volatility lineages mutate slower.

## 4. Visual System

### 4.1 Movesets

v4 already has a per-entity-class emoji map system. Each entity class (Baby, Adult, Elder) defines a static `emojiMap` that maps action names to emoji strings. v4.2 formalizes this as a **moveset** concept:

| Slot | Purpose | Example (Adult) | Example (Baby) | Example (Elder) |
|------|---------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Idle** | Default resting appearance | 🙂 | 👶 | 🧓 |
| **Action variants** | Per-action emoji | 😡 attacking, 😴 sleeping, 😋 eating, 😇 healing, 🤢 diseased | 👼 eating/washing | Same as adult, minus reproduce |

v4's existing emoji maps already follow this pattern — v4.2 simply adds the **animation layer** on top, so that actions without a dedicated emoji still get visual feedback.

**Key rule:** If no action emoji exists for an entity class + action combination, the agent keeps its idle emoji and relies on **animation** to indicate the action. This protects visual coherence.

### 4.2 Animations

Animations are the **universal action indicator** — they work for every agent regardless of entity class. They are the primary way the player identifies what an agent is doing. Action emojis (movesets) are the secondary, optional layer.

| Animation | Actions That Trigger It | Visual Effect |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------|
| **Shake** | Attack, taking damage | Rapid horizontal oscillation |
| **Bob** | Walking | Vertical bounce per step |
| **Pulse** | Healing, regenerating | Scale up/down cycle |
| **Bounce** | Reproducing, social success, leveling up | Vertical hop |
| **Shrink** | Dying, energy critically low | Progressive scale decrease |
| **Wiggle** | Eating, harvesting, drinking | Slight rotation oscillation |
| **Flash** | Level up, important event | Brief brightness burst |
| **Sway** | Sleeping | Gentle side-to-side drift |

Animations are **composable**: an agent can shake (attack animation) while displaying an angry emoji (attack moveset).

### 4.3 Indicators

Three indicator slots positioned around the agent emoji. Each displays a small emoji or icon. The player can **switch what each slot displays** via the UI control panel.

| Slot | Position | Default Display | Alternative Displays |
|------|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Top-left** | Above-left of base emoji | Faction flag 🚩 | Level number, lineage marker |
| **Top-right** | Above-right of base emoji | Pregnancy state 🥚 | Health band, energy band, mood icon |
| **Top-middle** | Directly above base emoji | (empty by default) | Alert state, need status, custom label |

Properties:
- Indicator emojis render at ~50% the size of the base agent emoji
- Slots can be empty (hidden) to reduce clutter
- Each slot's data source is independently switchable
- The UI provides a dropdown per slot to select what data to display
- New indicator types can be registered without code changes (data-driven)

### 4.4 Tools (Directional, Origin → Target)

Tools are small emojis rendered **between an agent and its target during a directed action**, oriented to point from origin to target. This preserves v4's existing tool rendering behavior.

The current v4 system already has directional tool rendering for two actions:
- 🗡️ (dagger) pointing from attacker to defender during attacks
- 🤚 (hand) pointing from harvester to harvested block during harvesting

**v4.2 generalizes this** without changing the visual logic:
- Each action definition in the action registry can declare a tool emoji
- When the action is executed against an **external-cell target** (a different cell containing another agent or block), the tool is rendered along the line between agent and target, oriented toward the target
- **Tools are rendered ONLY for actions whose `targetType === 'external_cell'`**
- For self-targeted actions (sleep, wash, eat from inventory), **no tool is rendered**. The action is communicated entirely through animation.

| Action | Tool Emoji | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Attack | 🗡️ | Points from attacker to defender (preserves v4 emoji) |
| Harvest | 🤚 | Points from harvester to tree/resource (preserves v4 emoji) |
| Build | 🔨 | Points from builder to construction site |
| Heal | 💊 | Points from healer to patient |
| Sleep, Wash, Eat (self) | (none) | Animation only |

Adding a new tool requires only a registry entry. The directional rendering logic is shared. The decision rule is in the action registry, not the renderer — the renderer simply asks "does this action have a tool and a directional target?" and renders accordingly.

## 5. Game Mechanics

### 5.1 Geographic Isolation Through Terrain

To enable allopatric speciation (populations diverging because they cannot interbreed across barriers), v4.2 expands the world generator to produce more **untraversable terrain**, naturally creating **isolated population pockets**.

- World generation should produce mountain ranges, walls of obstacles, water bodies, or other impassable features that divide the map into multiple connected regions
- Agents in different regions cannot interbreed (subject to mobility constraints)
- Over time, isolated populations diverge due to independent mutation and selection pressures
- This is a world-generation concern; mechanics for the existing pathfinding and grid systems do not change

The exact terrain generation algorithm is a TRD concern. Suggested approach: noise-based biome sketching with deliberate barrier placement.

### 5.2 Pregnancy and Need Transfer

Pregnancy in v4.2 introduces a **gradual need transfer** from parent to offspring during gestation. This replaces v4's simple "donated fullness" model.

Mechanic:
1. At conception, the offspring's DNA is determined (crossover + mutation) and the gestating agent enters the Pregnancy state.
2. The offspring begins with **all needs at zero** — fullness, hygiene, social.
3. Each tick during pregnancy, the parent transfers a small amount from each of its needs to the offspring's corresponding needs. The transfer rate is determined by the parent's Pregnancy expression (longer gestation = slower transfer = less burden per tick).
4. **The parent's needs decay at an increased rate** equal to the transfer rate, on top of its normal decay. A pregnant agent is genuinely metabolically taxed because it is feeding the offspring from its own reserves.
5. Pregnancy ends when either:
   - The offspring's needs reach a sufficient threshold (full birth — birth occurs)
   - The parent dies (offspring is lost; no birth)
6. At birth, the offspring is created at the parent's location with its accumulated need values as its starting needs — typically near full if pregnancy completed normally.

**Pregnancy = 0 (no AG gene or zero expression):** The offspring is born immediately at conception with **all needs at zero**. There are no "default starting needs" for organically born agents — the child will begin starving and must be fed by a parent (via Maternity) or find food quickly, or it will die. This makes Pregnancy expression a meaningful survival trait: lineages that invest in gestation produce healthier offspring; lineages that don't pay the cost of high infant mortality.

For agents with no AG gene, the existing v4 pregnancy model is used as a transitional fallback: pregnancy duration = `maturity.babyDurationMs × 0.5`, parents donate a random amount of fullness at conception (15–25 per parent), and birth occurs when the countdown timer expires. This fallback ensures backward compatibility but is superseded by the Pregnancy gene once present.

**Preserved from v4:** Existing miscarriage mechanics are retained — starvation (`fullness <= 0`) causes immediate miscarriage, and disease increases miscarriage risk proportionally. Pregnancy debuffs (60% attack, 1.5× fullness decay, 0.7× movement speed) continue to apply regardless of whether the v4 or v4.2 pregnancy model is active.

### 5.3 Leveling and Energy Cost Scaling

Agents with Aptitude > 0 have an XP system. Each level grants stat increases (HP, attack, energy capacity), but **also increases the energy cost of every action** by a small amount.

This is the trade-off for the Aptitude trait: leveling fast is metabolically expensive. An agent with Aptitude near 0 levels slowly but pays normal energy costs forever. An agent with maxed Aptitude levels rapidly to high stats, but its food requirements grow proportionally.

The exact curve (linear vs exponential, percent vs flat) is a TRD concern. The principle is: leveling provides power AND cost in lockstep, so high-Aptitude agents cannot dominate by leveling alone — they must also evolve adequate metabolism and foraging efficiency.

## 6. Design Decisions

### 6.1 Size and Grid Cells

**One agent per cell** regardless of any future Size trait. Large agents may render visibly larger within their cell (visual scaling), but spatial occupation is unchanged.

## 7. Backward Compatibility

v4.2 maintains backward compatibility with v4.x save files:

- **DNA format is unchanged.** Existing genomes are valid. New gene codes (AD, AG, AP) produce default expression values when absent from existing DNA.
- **Agent structure is extended, not broken.** New fields (e.g., pregnancy transfer state) have sensible defaults that match v4 behavior.
- **Existing trait expression is widened.** Hard ceilings are removed, but floors are preserved. Agents in existing saves whose DNA caused trait values to hit a ceiling (e.g., Strength clamped at max 18, Resilience at max 180) will now express higher values upon re-expression. This is the intended balancing effect — but it means loaded agents may be slightly stronger than when they were saved. The new cost functions offset this by making those higher values more expensive to maintain.
- **Gregariousness (OO) → Sociality (AD) migration.** Existing saves with OO genes continue to function; the implementation must map OO to the Sociality behavior. New agents are seeded with AD.
- **Visual system additions are purely additive.** Animations, indicators, and tool generalization layer on top of the existing renderer without breaking existing display.
- **Save format versioning.** The persistence layer gains an optional version marker. Files without a marker are treated as v4.x and loaded with default values for new fields.
